## Penn Blockchain Conference

I spent the weekend in Philadelphia for the Penn Blockchain Conference. I'm still pretty new to blockchain, so I spent the weekend trying to absorb as much information as I could and made a list of things I wanted to learn more about.

- Learn about beta neutral and delta neutral trading strategies
- Understand perpetuals and how to use the basis trade
- Research Terra and using algorithms to peg stable coins
- Try using smart contracts to simulate Strategy's bitcoin products like STRC
- Spend some time to read various VC theses

Although I usually see myself going into more of a developer, the financial modeling of crypto really fascinates me, and I look forward to trading some myself.

## Polymarket Bots

There are so many people on X who won't stop talking about their Polymarket bots that I just have to try it out. Here is what I'm going to try:

- Extreme price targets — buy things at above 88%
- Making sports models, and buying/selling when your model shows something more than 5% off
- Bayesian news analysis — for example, if a star player goes down on the field the odds change against that team, however it is not known as fact that the player is out for the whole game. There is a percentage related to whether the player is actually down, and the associated percentages if it is true
- Use Kelly Criterion to figure out how much to risk
- Beta neutral arbitrage on related markets
